# Causal Relations

Use this reference to explain the chapter's basic understanding of causality.

## Why Causal Relations Matter

When researchers choose a research direction, they have already begun asking how to answer a causal question. A causal explanation is not just naming a factor; it must clarify:

- What is the cause?
- What is the outcome?
- Why does the cause lead to the outcome?
- How does the cause lead to the outcome?
- Under what conditions does the explanation hold?

## Regularity View

The regularity view emphasizes patterned conjunction between cause and outcome.

Core idea:

```text
Cause and outcome appear in a regular relation.
The cause precedes the outcome.
Changes in the cause and outcome show patterned co-variation.
```

Two forms:

- Deterministic causal statement: the presence of the cause necessarily leads to the outcome.
- Probabilistic causal statement: changes in the cause increase or decrease the probability of the outcome.

Guardrail: regular association does not by itself prove causation.

## Counterfactual View

The counterfactual view emphasizes the outcome's dependence on the cause.

Core idea:

```text
If the cause had not occurred, or had taken a different value, the outcome would not have occurred or would have changed.
```

Guardrail: the counterfactual outcome cannot be directly observed. It must be constructed reasonably.

## Mechanism View

The mechanism view emphasizes the process through which the cause produces the outcome.

Core idea:

```text
Cause -> connected entities and activities -> outcome
```

In international relations, mechanisms often involve states, leaders, alliances, organizations, domestic groups, firms, publics, perceptions, incentives, capabilities, information, legitimacy, institutions, and interactions.

Guardrail: a timeline is not automatically a mechanism. The link between steps must be causal.

## Effects-Of-Causes And Causes-Of-Effects

### Effects of causes

Question:

```text
What effect does a given cause have on an outcome?
```

This orientation asks whether X affects Y and, when possible, how much or with what probability.

### Causes of effects

Question:

```text
What caused a particular outcome?
```

This orientation reconstructs the causal chain behind an observed result.

## Practical Diagnosis

| Question | Likely causal orientation |
|---|---|
| Does X increase the probability of Y? | Regularity / effects of causes |
| Would Y have happened without X? | Counterfactual |
| How did X produce Y? | Mechanism |
| Why did this particular Y occur? | Causes of effects |
| Does X matter across many cases? | Effects of causes |
